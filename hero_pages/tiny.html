    Grows in size and strength as battle ensues.
    A curious being of animated stone, Tiny roams the world in search of his origins. Seemingly mild-mannered, the Stone Giant is nonetheless a force to behold as he brings down a multitude of Avalanches. He Tosses friends and foes alike across the battlefield, landing them on top of enemies and into the fray. Growing to tremendous size and strength as the battle progresses, Tiny's power can quickly become terrifying. Some have even claimed to witness the Stone Giant Wielding a Massive Tree, bludgeoning and Volleying Trees at a fortress until only rubble remained.

    Upgrades[edit]

    Aghanim's[edit]

    Aghanim's Scepter Upgrade:
    New Ability
    Grants the Tree Volley ability.

    Tiny channels to throw random trees within the radius towards the targeted area that deals Tiny's attack damage to enemies in that area with each tree thrown.

    Each tree tossed causes Tiny to instant attack affected units, applying various attack effects.
    Aghanim's Shard Upgrade:
    No longer has a number of attack limit. All Tree Grab effects are permanent on Tiny until he throws the tree out.
    Increases the damage of the thrown tree projectile.

    Bio[edit]

    My enemies break upon me like surf upon the stone.
    ▶️  Tiny, the Stone Giant
    Alias:
    Stone Giant
    Lore:
    Coming to life as a chunk of stone, Tiny's origins are a mystery on which he continually speculates. He is a Stone Giant now, but what did he used to be? A splinter broken from a Golem's heel? A shard swept from a gargoyle-sculptor's workshop? A fragment of the Oracular Visage of Garthos? A deep curiosity drives him, and he travels the world tirelessly seeking his origins, his parentage, his people. As he roams, he gathers weight and size; the forces that weather lesser rocks, instead cause Tiny to grow and ever grow.
    Voice:
    Eric Newsome (Responses)

    • Replaces Tree Grab until Tiny loses the tree.
    • Tree Throw has two targeting functionalities, which modifies the properties of the tree projectile:
      • When unit-targeted:
        • Homes in on the target, and hits the target upon reaching it.
        • Does not provide vision while traveling to the target in this way.
        • Cannot target enemy Couriers.
      • When ground-targeted:
        • Travels in a straight line up to its max travel distance, then disappears.
        • Has a 300-radius collision size, the tree projectile may hit another enemy unit before reaching the ground-targeted point.
        • Grants a 200-radius flying vision around the projectile, up to 1.33 seconds, while traveling.
    • Grants a 2-second 200-radius flying vision at its final localtion of the tree projectile.
    • Affects enemy units up 1500 range away with the travel distance and collision radius.
    • Tree Throw's instant attack applied on the targeted unit, or the first enemy it hits, has the following properties:
      • The instant attack applies splash damage around the target, and its damage instance is defined as:
        • EXPR
          InstantAtk DamageInstance = ΣAtkDmg + 20 OR 120
      • Procs any attack modifiers and on-cast effects as per normal.
        • Specifically for cleave and splash sources, the effects are based on the affected enemy target's position, damaging a trapezoid area in the opposite direction where the projectile came from.
        • However, splash source centers the splash damage around the first unit it hits instead.
      • Have True Strike.
      • Not affected by disarm sources.
    • Enemy units around the primary attacked target are then affected by the splash damage, that deal physical spell damage and has the following properties:
      • Splash damage is based on Tiny's attack damage value.
      • Affected by damage block sources on the enemy individually within the area-of-effect.
      • Has the following damage flags:
        • No-spell-lifesteal
        • No-spell-amplification
        • The flags prevent the splash damage from interacting with their respective sources.
      • Stack and work independently with other cleave sources.
      • Affects the following units:
        • Enemy Couriers.
    • Tree Throw first applies the slow debuff, then the instant attack and splash damage.
    • Although the tree thrown is a projectile and it may look like a ranged attack, the attack still counts as a melee attack when coming from Tiny.
      • This means all abilities that use different values for melee and ranged heroes (e.g. Skull Basher) treat the caster as a melee hero.
    • Ranged heroes' instant attacks do not use attack projectiles and are considered as ranged attacks.
    • ABILITY DISJOINT
      Only the unit-targeted projectile can be disjointed and nullifies the ability effects.
    • SPELL STEAL
      No longer can throw the wielded tree upon losing the ability.
    • SOULBIND
      Only affects the primary target.

    Trivia[edit]

    • Tiny's quote "▶️ Tiny Smash!" is a reference to Hulk, a well-known superhero from the Marvel Universe.
    • Tree Grab is still called "Craggy Exterior" after his old passive ability in the game files.
    • Tree Throw's slow debuff does not have its own particle system and uses the particle effects of Thunder Clap's slow debuff instead.
    • Warcraft 3 similarities:
      • Tiny's hero concept is originally based on the Mountain Giants, a large Night Elf unit.
        • Although based on the Mountain Giants, Tiny's title was slightly changed to Stone Giant, which is now also used in Dota 2.
        • Tree Grab's original iteration was identical to War Club.
        • Stone Giant is still Tiny's official title in Dota 2, with Tiny being his name.
      • Avalanche's core ability was based on the Tinker's Cluster Rockets, a channeled area ability that deals damage and mini-stuns.
